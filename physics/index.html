Class 10 Physics - Notes with Detailed Ray Diagram Data

1. Reflection of Light (Mirrors)

Laws & Sign Convention

Laws of Reflection:

The angle of incidence ($\angle i$) is equal to the angle of reflection ($\angle r$). $$ \angle i = \angle r $$

(This concept represents the path of light hitting a surface and bouncing off, with the angles measured from the Normal.)

New Cartesian Sign Convention:
  • All distances are measured from the **Pole (P)**.
  • The object is always placed to the **left** of the mirror.
  • Distances measured in the direction of Incident Light (Right) are **Positive**.
  • Distances measured against the direction of Incident Light (Left) are **Negative**.
  • Heights measured upwards (above principal axis) are **Positive**, and downwards are **Negative**.

Convex Mirror Image Formation (Always Virtual)

Convex mirrors are **Diverging Mirrors**. The image formation is highly consistent:

Convex Mirror Image Summary:
  • **Object Position:** Anywhere between $\infty$ and P.
  • **Image Position:** Always **Behind the Mirror** (between P and F).
  • **Image Size:** Always **Diminished**.
  • **Nature:** Always **Virtual** and **Erect**.
Mirror Formulae & Magnification

Mirror Formula:

$$ \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} $$

Magnification ($m$):

$$ m = \frac{h'}{h} = - \frac{v}{u} $$

2. Refraction of Light (Lenses)

Refractive Index & Snell's Law

Refraction is the bending of light when it passes from one medium to another.

Snell's Law:

$$ \frac{\sin i}{\sin r} = n_{21} $$

Bending Rules (Visual Description):

  • **Rare $\to$ Dense Medium:** The light ray **bends TOWARDS the Normal**. (e.g., Air to Glass)
  • **Dense $\to$ Rare Medium:** The light ray **bends AWAY from the Normal**. (e.g., Glass to Air)

Concave Lens Image Formation (Always Virtual)

Concave lenses are **Diverging Lenses**. The image formation is highly consistent:

Concave Lens Image Summary:
  • **Object Position:** Anywhere between $\infty$ and Optical Center (O).
  • **Image Position:** Always **Between O and $F_1$** (on the same side as Object).
  • **Image Size:** Always **Diminished**.
  • **Nature:** Always **Virtual** and **Erect**.
Lens Formulae & Power of Lens

Lens Formula:

$$ \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} $$

Magnification ($m$):

$$ m = \frac{h'}{h} = \frac{v}{u} $$

Power of a Lens ($P$):

$$ P = \frac{1}{f \text{ (in meters)}} $$
